The 6 Hard Questions We Asked Every Candidate

We did not take anyone's word for anything. We ran every serious AIOS candidate through the same six questions. Most failed immediately. Only one passed all six.

Question 1 — Is AI baked into the OS layer, or bolted on top?
Every incumbent OS (Windows, macOS, Linux, Android) treats AI as an application layer. A real AI OS means AI is the runtime itself — not a process you launch, not a plugin, not a service. We found exactly one system where agents run natively at the OS architecture level.
Question 2 — Does it make decisions, or just generate text?
Generating text is not intelligence. Intelligence is making consequential decisions with accountability. We required: dual-verified decision-making where two independent poles must agree before any critical action is taken. No single point of failure. No hallucination-driven action.
Question 3 — Can it heal itself, or does it need human intervention?
A real AI OS watches its own health 24/7, catches errors before they cascade, and recovers automatically. We required demonstrated self-healing with logged recovery strategies. Not marketing copy — actual logs, actual recovery.
Question 4 — Is there a verifiable architecture, or just a pitch deck?
We required a published, machine-verifiable canonical architecture signature. Something a developer could call an API and confirm in real time. Almost every competitor had a deck. One had a live cryptographic lattice we could query directly.
Question 5 — Who is behind it, and are they accountable?
Anonymous teams, offshore shell projects, and pivoting startups fail this test immediately. We required a traceable founding team with skin in the game and a product history you can audit.
Question 6 — Does it have a future, or is it already at a ceiling?
A real AI OS must be self-evolving by design — not feature-frozen. It must have a learning model, an expansion path, and a lattice that grows. One system had a published 480-node harmonic expansion path. The rest had a Trello board.
